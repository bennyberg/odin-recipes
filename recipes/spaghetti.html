One-Pot Spaghetti Recipe

Description

Here’s a One-Pot Spaghetti recipe that’s quick, cozy, and requires minimal cleanup.

Ingredients

  • 300 g (10–11 oz) dry spaghetti
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400 g / 14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 3 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • Salt & black pepper, to taste
  • 1 handful fresh basil (or parsley), chopped
  • Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or deep pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until soft (3–4 minutes). Add garlic and cook another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Pour in diced tomatoes, broth, oregano, chili flakes, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine. Add the dry spaghetti, making sure it’s mostly submerged in liquid.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cook uncovered, stirring often so pasta doesn’t stick together, for about 12–14 minutes or until pasta is al dente and liquid has mostly absorbed (add a splash of water if it dries too quickly).
  4. Stir in fresh basil or parsley. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  5. Plate hot and top with parmesan if desired.
